Introduction to Programming

Beginner

10 Weeks

Introduction to Programming

  • Our Introduction to Programming course is designed to teach middle school and high school students how to create useful applications as they see fit, even without prior programming knowledge.
  • Students will meet with mentors once weekly for a one hour session over ten weeks.
  • Students can choose different timings as fits their schedule.
  • These sessions will include information, discussion, and time for students to create their own programs based on what they want.

Course Layout Information

  • Lessons 1-3 go over how programming works, variables types, and console IO/ manipulation.
  • Lessons 4-6 go over mathematical operations in programming, conditional logic, and control structures.
  • Lessons 7-9 go over data structures, functions, and methods to debug python code.
  • Lesson 10 goes over problem solving with programming
  • After the course is a 2 day mini-hackathon with workshops and prizes.

Course Prerequisites

  • Students will need a discord account as we will be communicating most of our important information through the platform. Our lessons will take place on discord.
  • Students will need internet access during our course because our lessons will be live. If they wish to verbally ask questions, they will need a working mic, though it is not a requirement since they can still type their questions.
  • Rather than download software, students will use repl.it, a free online browser IDE. We will show students how to set it up in the course, though directions are available here.

Pygame Workshop

Moderate

2.5 Hours

Pygame Python Workshop

  • Our Pygame workshop will guide students through the process of coding a 2D platformer game in Python. Prior knowledge of Python is highly recommended.
  • Mentors will guide the students to complete the game development in a single 2.5 hour session.
  • Students will learn many of the game development fundamentals and various python concepts.
  • All assets are provided and students will be taken through each step of the process collaboratively.
