Introduction to Programming

  • Our Introduction to Programming course is designed to teach middle school and high school students how to create useful applications as they see fit, even without prior programming knowledge.
  • Students will meet with mentors for one hour sessions on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays, over the course of 4 weeks.
  • Each session will take place from 2:00 pm to 3:00 pm EST
  • These sessions will include information, discussion, and time for students to create their own programs based on what they want.

Course Layout Information

  • Week 1 goes over how programming works, variables (types: integer, float, boolean, string), and console IO and manipulation.
  • Week 2 goes over basic mathematical operations in programming, conditional logic, and control structures (loops, if/else).
  • Week 3 goes over data structures (python lists), functions, and methods to debug python code.
  • Week 4 goes over problem solving with programming and a 2 day mini-hackathon with workshops and prizes.

Course Prerequisites

  • Students will need a discord account as we will be communicating most of our important information through the platform. Our lessons will take place on discord.
  • Students will need internet access during our course because our lessons will be live. If they wish to verbally ask questions, they will need a working mic, though it is not a requirement since they can still type their questions.
  • Rather than download software, students will use repl.it, a free online browser IDE. We will show students how to set it up in the course, though directions are available here.
